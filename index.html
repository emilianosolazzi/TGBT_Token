About the Protocol

Temporal Gradient is a proof-of-work randomness and anchoring protocol deployed on Arbitrum One. Independent miners perform CPU-bound work, submit commit-reveal solutions to an on-chain MiningModule, and are rewarded in TGBT when their solutions are validated. Accepted solutions feed a verifiable randomness beacon and a batch-epoch Merkle commitment system.

The protocol additionally harvests entropy from Bitcoin stale blocks and creates Bitcoin dead-UTXO anchors for provenance records.

TGBT's role within the protocol:

  • Reward asset for accepted mining solutions (MiningModule → TokenomicsModuleV2.onBlockMined()).
  • Reward asset for validated Bitcoin stale-block proofs (StaleBlockOracle.claimReward()).
  • Payment asset for the proof marketplace and the dead-UTXO certificate registry.

The TGBT token contract is non-proxy, non-upgradeable, and non-pausable. Only the currently authorized TokenomicsModuleV2 can call mint(), constrained by immutable allocation budgets encoded in the module.
